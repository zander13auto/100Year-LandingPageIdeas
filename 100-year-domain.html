Everything you need to know

Common questions.

Answers about eligibility, succession, refunds, and what happens to your domain if something happens to you.

Which top-level domains are eligible for the 100-Year Domain?

Four TLDs: .com, .org, .net, and .blog. We selected these because their registries have the strongest track record of operational continuity and the clearest contractual paths for multi-decade registration.

Country-code TLDs (e.g. .uk, .io) and newer brand TLDs aren't currently eligible because their registry policies can change in ways that would jeopardize the 100-year guarantee.

How does a domain actually get renewed for 100 years?

ICANN limits single-shot domain registrations to ten years. The Century Proxy registers your domain for the maximum ten-year term immediately, then every year it adds another year back on, so there are always at least nine years of buffer in front of you.

That means the proxy could miss a year of renewals without your domain ever being at risk. It would have to miss nine consecutive years before anything broke — and the Long-Term Investment exists specifically to make sure that never happens.

What happens to my domain if I become unreachable?

The Special Trust holds your succession instructions — not Automattic, and not a single hosting account that could be lost. You designate a beneficiary when you register; you can update the designation any time over the next century.

If we can't reach you and the beneficiary contacts the trust with reasonable proof of authority, the trust executes the transfer — no probate scramble, no support-ticket lottery.

Can I point the domain at a host that isn't WordPress.com?

Yes. You retain full DNS control — A, AAAA, CNAME, MX, TXT, SRV, CAA, NS, the full set. Point it at WordPress.com, point it at AWS, point it at a static site host. The 100-Year Domain is about preserving the address, not locking the destination.

What's the refund policy?

120 days from purchase, full refund. Past 120 days, the domain registration and trust paperwork are in motion, so a partial refund may be possible depending on the stage — contact support and we'll work through it.

Can I add more domains later?

Yes. Each 100-Year Domain is a separate one-time purchase. Many holders register a small portfolio at once — personal name, organization name, project name — to lock in the address space across a century.

What about email on the domain?

You control the MX records, so you can point email at Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Fastmail, Proton, or any other provider. The proxy never reads or routes your email — it only handles the registration mechanics.

What if Automattic isn't here in 100 years?

Reasonable question. Only about 0.5% of companies founded today will still exist in 100 years. The 100-Year Domain was structured so Automattic's continued existence isn't required — the Special Trust, Century Proxy, and Long-Term Investment are held outside Automattic, by independent parties with contractual obligations to domain holders.

If Automattic is still here (we plan to be), we keep running it. If not, the proxy and investment carry the domain to its century mark on their own.
