Honest answer

What if Automattic isn't here in 100 years?

Reasonable question. Most companies don't last a century — only about 0.5% of US companies founded today will still exist in 100 years.

That's exactly why the 100-Year Plan was structured so that Automattic's continued existence isn't required. The Special Trust, Century Proxy, and Long-Term Investment are deliberately held outside of Automattic — by independent parties with contractual obligations to plan-holders, not to us.

If we're still around (we plan to be), great — we keep running it. If we're not, the proxy and investment do. Either way, your site stays yours.

Everything you need to know

Common questions.

Answers about the structure, the price, the succession path, and what happens if any single piece of the system fails.

What does $38,000 actually pay for?

A century of managed WordPress.com hosting, domain registration with renewal handled by the Century Proxy, continuous security updates, format migrations as the web evolves, and concierge support — for you and every successor.

About 12% of the price is set aside in the Long-Term Investment account. Its returns finance the operational costs for the remaining decades, which is the part that makes the 100-year guarantee real instead of theoretical.

What if Automattic isn't here in 100 years?

Only about 0.5% of companies founded today will still exist in 100 years — we're aware. That's why the Plan was structured so Automattic's continued existence isn't required.

The Special Trust, Century Proxy, and Long-Term Investment are held outside Automattic, by independent parties with contractual obligations to plan-holders. If we're here (we plan to be), we keep running it. If not, the proxy and investment carry the site to its century mark.

Can I transfer the plan to someone else?

Yes. You name a beneficiary at purchase — and you can change the designation any time during your life. If you can't be reached, the Special Trust executes the transfer to the named beneficiary, without a probate scramble or a support-ticket lottery.

What happens to my site if I die before the 100 years are up?

The Special Trust executes your succession instructions. The site stays online; the domain stays registered; the named beneficiary becomes the new owner of record. They inherit the same century of managed hosting, the same concierge support, and the same beneficiary-designation rights for the remainder of the term.

What's the refund policy?

120 days from purchase, full refund — no questions. After 120 days the trust paperwork and investment funding are in motion, so a partial refund may be possible depending on the stage. Contact support and we'll walk through it.

Can I still update the design and content of my site?

Yes — it's a normal WordPress.com site with the full block editor, themes, plugins, custom code, and the AI Site Editor. The Plan is about keeping it online for a century. The day-to-day of writing, designing, and publishing works exactly like any other WordPress.com site.

What does "format migrations" mean?

Today's media formats won't be tomorrow's. JPEG, MP4, WebP, AVIF — each one will eventually be superseded. When that happens, we migrate your content into whatever the current standard is, so a visitor in 2086 can still actually view what you published in 2026.

Can I add more 100-Year Plans or 100-Year Domains later?

Yes. Each is a separate one-time purchase. Some holders bundle multiple domains around a single Plan; others operate several Plans across different sites. If you only need the domain piece without the hosting, the 100-Year Domain covers that on its own.

How is the Long-Term Investment managed?

Conservatively, in a segregated account held outside Automattic, with returns designated solely for plan-holder operations. The fund is independently audited, and we publish a public continuity report on a defined cadence so plan-holders can verify the math is still working.
